The general representation for the transformation $\bar{x}$ of the coordinate $x$ is given by a one-parameter group $\bar{x}(\epsilon)$ acting on the initial point $x$. The transformation depends on the constants $c_1, c_2, c_A$ which define a specific infinitesimal generator from the symmetry algebra.

There are two forms for the transformation, depending on the value of the constant $c_1$ (related to time translation):

**Form 1: For transformations involving a non-trivial time shift ($c_1 \neq 0$)**
The transformation is given by:
$\bar{x} = x + c_2\epsilon - \frac{2c_A e^{k_1 t}}{k_1^2 c_1} (e^{k_1 c_1 \epsilon} - 1)$

**Form 2: For transformations that do not shift time ($c_1 = 0$)**
The transformation simplifies to a linear shift in $x$:
$\bar{x} = x + \left( c_2 - \frac{2c_A}{k_1}e^{k_1 t} \right)\epsilon$

Here, $\epsilon$ is the group parameter, and $c_1, c_2, c_A$ are arbitrary constants that specify the particular symmetry transformation.
