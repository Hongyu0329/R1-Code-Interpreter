
The common eigenvalues are found by first identifying the possible total angular momentum quantum numbers, $j$, which are $j = 3/2$ and $j = 1/2$. The eigenvalues are then calculated for each case.

**1. For the j = 3/2 multiplet:**
*   The single eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$.
*   The four corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$, and $-\frac{3}{2}\hbar$.

**2. For the j = 1/2 multiplet:**
*   The single eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$.
*   The two corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$ and $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$.
