**Important Interaction with Excedrin**

*   The most important thing we need to discuss is the over-the-counter Excedrin you took. Excedrin contains aspirin, which is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug, or NSAID. Your prescription antidepressant, Fluoxetine, can interact with NSAIDs like aspirin.
*   Taking these two medications together significantly increases the risk of bleeding, especially in your stomach and intestines. This can be serious. Signs of this could include easy bruising, nosebleeds, or more seriously, black, tarry stools or stomach pain that doesn't go away.
*   For future headaches, it would be safer to choose a product that only contains acetaminophen (like Tylenol) and does not contain any NSAIDs like aspirin, ibuprofen, or naproxen. I recommend you talk with your doctor about the safest and most effective way to manage your headaches moving forward.

I also want to go over a few key points for each of your prescribed medications:
