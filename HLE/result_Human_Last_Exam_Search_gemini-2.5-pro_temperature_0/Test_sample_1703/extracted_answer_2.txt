The area of the triangle \( T(t) \) as a function of time \( t \) is given by the formula:
\[ A(t) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} \left(225 + 3 \cdot d(t)^2\right) \]
where \( d(t) \) is a periodic function of time representing the distance of each vertex of the triangle from the midpoint of its respective side of the hexagon. This distance function has a period of 10 seconds.

Let \( t' = t \pmod{10} \). The function \( d(t)^2 \) is defined as:
\[ d(t)^2 = \begin{cases} (t')^2 & \text{if } 0 \le t' \le 5 \\ (10-t')^2 & \text{if } 5 < t' \le 10 \end{cases} \]

Therefore, the area \( A(t) \) can be written piece-wise for a cycle of 10 seconds:
\[ A(t) = \begin{cases} \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} (225 + 3(t')^2) & \text{if } 0 \le t' \le 5 \\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4} (225 + 3(10-t')^2) & \text{if } 5 < t' \le 10 \end{cases} \]
The rotation of the hexagon does not affect the area of the triangle.
