The general representation for the infinitesimal transformation on x is $\xi(t) = C_1 + C_2 e^{k_1 t}$, where $C_1$ and $C_2$ are arbitrary constants. The finite transformations are given by the flow of the corresponding generators. For a transformation group element parameterized by $\epsilon$, and defined by constants $c_1, c_3, c_5$, the transformation on $x$ is:
1. If $c_5 \neq 0$: $x^* = x + c_3 \epsilon - \frac{2c_1}{k_1^2 c_5}e^{k_1 t} (e^{k_1 c_5 \epsilon} - 1)$
2. If $c_5 = 0$: $x^* = x + (c_3 - \frac{2c_1}{k_1} e^{k_1 t}) \epsilon$
