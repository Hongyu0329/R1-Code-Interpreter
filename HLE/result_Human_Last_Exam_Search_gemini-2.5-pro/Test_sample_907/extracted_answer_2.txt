Here are the equations for the absorption cross-section under the specified conditions.

**a) Neglecting interaction between molecules:**

The absorption cross-section for a chain of N non-interacting molecules is:

σₐ(ω₀) = (N√π τ / (ε₀cnħ)) Σᵢ,₟ ω₟ᵢ |**d**₟ᵢ · **e**|² exp[-(ω₟ᵢ - ω₀)²τ²] f(Eᵢ) [1 - f(E₟)]

*   **N**: Number of molecules
*   **τ**: Pulse duration parameter
*   **ε₀**: Vacuum permittivity
*   **c**: Speed of light
*   **n**: Refractive index
*   **ħ**: Reduced Planck constant
*   **i, f**: Initial and final molecular orbitals
*   **ω₟ᵢ**: Transition frequency (E₟ - Eᵢ)/ħ
*   **d**₟ᵢ: Transition dipole moment
*   **e**: Laser polarization vector
*   **ω₀**: Laser carrier frequency
*   **f(E)**: Fermi-Dirac distribution function

**b) Including near-neighbor interaction:**

The absorption cross-section for a chain with near-neighbor interactions, leading to electronic bands, is:

σₙ(ω₀) = (L√π τ / (ε₀cnħa)) ∫ [dk/(2π)] ω𝒸ᵥ(k) |**d**𝒸ᵥ(k) · **e**|² exp[-(ω𝒸ᵥ(k) - ω₀)²τ²] f(Eᵥ(k)) [1 - f(E𝒸(k))]

*   **L**: Length of the chain
*   **a**: Intermolecular distance
*   **∫ [dk/(2π)]**: Integral over the first Brillouin zone
*   **v, c**: Valence and conduction bands
*   **Eᵥ(k), E𝒸(k)**: Energy dispersion of the bands
*   **ω𝒸ᵥ(k)**: k-dependent transition frequency
*   **d**𝒸ᵥ(k): k-dependent transition dipole moment
*   Other symbols have the same meaning as in part (a).
