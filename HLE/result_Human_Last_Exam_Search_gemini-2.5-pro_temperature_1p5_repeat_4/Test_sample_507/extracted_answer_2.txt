Two languages from two different Asian cultures with no direct contact where the words for "mom" and "dad" are nearly identical or highly similar are Malayalam and Tagalog. In Malayalam, "mom" is "Amma" and "dad" is "Appan". In Tagalog, common terms for "mom" and "dad" are "Mama" and "Papa", respectively. The word for "broom" in a dialect of Malayalam is "machi", while in Tagalog it is "walis", which are not similar.
