The problem has two sets of solutions corresponding to the two possible values of the total angular momentum quantum number, $j$.
1. For the $j=3/2$ multiplet, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$. The corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$, and $-\frac{3}{2}\hbar$.
2. For the $j=1/2$ multiplet, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$. The corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$ and $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$.
