The Poynting vector $\vec{S}$ in cylindrical coordinates $(r, \phi, z)$ is:
For inside the rod ($r \le R$):
$\vec{S}(r) = - \frac{E \rho v r}{2} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v r^2}{4 \epsilon_0} \hat{z}$
For outside the rod ($r > R$):
$\vec{S}(r) = - \frac{E \rho v R^2}{2 r} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v R^4}{4 \epsilon_0 r^2} \hat{z}$
where $E$ is the external electric field strength, $\rho$ is the charge density, $v$ is the rod's speed, $R$ is the rod's radius, $r$ is the radial distance from the axis, and $\epsilon_0$ is the permittivity of free space.
