The common eigenvalues are found by considering the two possible values for the total angular momentum quantum number, j = 3/2 and j = 1/2.
For j = 3/2, the eigenvalue of J^2 is 15/4 * h_bar^2. The corresponding eigenvalues of J_z are 3/2 * h_bar, 1/2 * h_bar, -1/2 * h_bar, and -3/2 * h_bar. This gives the pairs: (15/4 * h_bar^2, 3/2 * h_bar), (15/4 * h_bar^2, 1/2 * h_bar), (15/4 * h_bar^2, -1/2 * h_bar), and (15/4 * h_bar^2, -3/2 * h_bar).
For j = 1/2, the eigenvalue of J^2 is 3/4 * h_bar^2. The corresponding eigenvalues of J_z are 1/2 * h_bar and -1/2 * h_bar. This gives the pairs: (3/4 * h_bar^2, 1/2 * h_bar) and (3/4 * h_bar^2, -1/2 * h_bar).
