For a p-electron ($l=1$, $s=1/2$), there are two possible values for the total angular momentum quantum number, $j$: $1/2$ and $3/2$.

The common eigenvalues of $J^2$ and $J_z$ are determined for each value of $j$.

For $j = 1/2$:
*   The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $j(j+1)\hbar^2 = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{2}+1)\hbar^2 = \frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$.
*   The eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $m_j\hbar$, where $m_j = -1/2, 1/2$. So the eigenvalues are $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$ and $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$.

For $j = 3/2$:
*   The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $j(j+1)\hbar^2 = \frac{3}{2}(\frac{3}{2}+1)\hbar^2 = \frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$.
*   The eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $m_j\hbar$, where $m_j = -3/2, -1/2, 1/2, 3/2$. So the eigenvalues are $-\frac{3}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar, \frac{1}{2}\hbar,$ and $\frac{3}{2}\hbar$.
