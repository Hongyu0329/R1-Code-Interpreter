Here are the final equations for the absorption cross-section:

**a) Without Intermolecular Interaction:**
The total absorption cross-section for a chain of N non-interacting, randomly oriented molecules is:
$$ \sigma_{total}(\omega_L) = N \left( \frac{\pi \omega_{eg} |\boldsymbol{\mu}_{eg}|^2}{3 \hbar c \epsilon_0} \right) \left( \frac{\tau}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \exp\left(-\frac{(\omega_L - \omega_{eg})^2 \tau^2}{2}\right) \right) $$
where $\omega_{eg}$ is the single-molecule transition frequency and $\boldsymbol{\mu}_{eg}$ is the single-molecule transition dipole moment.

**b) With Near-Neighbor Interaction:**
The absorption cross-section for a chain of N interacting molecules is:
$$ \sigma_{chain}(\omega_L) = \left( \frac{\pi \omega_{k=0} N |\boldsymbol{\mu}_{eg}|^2}{3 \hbar c \epsilon_0} \right) \left( \frac{\tau}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \exp\left(-\frac{(\omega_L - \omega_{k=0})^2 \tau^2}{2}\right) \right) $$
where $\omega_{k=0}$ is the transition frequency to the k=0 Frenkel exciton state, given by $\omega_{k=0} = \omega_{eg} + (\Delta E + 2J)/\hbar$.
