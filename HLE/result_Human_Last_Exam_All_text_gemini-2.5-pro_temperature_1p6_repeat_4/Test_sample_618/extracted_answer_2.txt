The general form of the transformation on the coordinate $x$ is given by $\bar{x}(\epsilon) = \int_{0}^{\epsilon} \xi(\bar{t}(s)) ds + x$, where $\bar{t}(s) = t + c_2 s$. There are two distinct forms depending on whether the constant $c_2$ (from the time-translation part of the symmetry generator) is zero or non-zero. Let $\epsilon$ be the group parameter.

Case 1: For subgroups with time translation ($c_2 \neq 0$):
$\bar{x} = x + c_4 \epsilon + \frac{c_3}{k_1^2 c_2} e^{k_1 t} (e^{k_1 c_2 \epsilon} - 1)$

Case 2: For subgroups without time translation ($c_2 = 0$):
$\bar{x} = x + \left(\frac{c_3}{k_1} e^{k_1 t} + c_4\right) \epsilon$
