In cylindrical coordinates, the Poynting vector is:
For $r < R$ (inside the rod):
$$ \vec{S} = - \frac{E \rho v r}{2} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v r^2}{4\epsilon_0} \hat{k} $$
For $r > R$ (outside the rod):
$$ \vec{S} = - \frac{E \rho v R^2}{2r} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v R^4}{4\epsilon_0 r^2} \hat{k} $$
where $r$ is the radial distance from the axis of the rod, and $\hat{r}$ and $\hat{k}$ are the radial and axial unit vectors, respectively.
