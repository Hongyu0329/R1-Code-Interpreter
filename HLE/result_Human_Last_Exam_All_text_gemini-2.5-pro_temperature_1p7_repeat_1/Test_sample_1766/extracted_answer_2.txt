The common eigenvalues are pairs of (Eigenvalue of $J^2$, Eigenvalue of $J_z$). There are two sets of solutions corresponding to the two possible values of the total angular momentum quantum number, $j$.

For the state with $j=3/2$, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$. The possible common eigenvalues are:
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{3}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar)$

For the state with $j=1/2$, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$. The possible common eigenvalues are:
*   $(\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
