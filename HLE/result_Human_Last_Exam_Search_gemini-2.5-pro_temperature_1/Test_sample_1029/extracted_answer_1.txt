The Poynting vector $\vec{S}$ is different inside and outside the cylindrical rod.

**Inside the rod (for $r < R$):**
$$ \vec{S}_{in} = -\frac{E\rho v r}{2} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v r^2}{4\epsilon_0} \hat{z} $$

**Outside the rod (for $r > R$):**
$$ \vec{S}_{out} = -\frac{E\rho v R^2}{2r} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v R^4}{4\epsilon_0 r^2} \hat{z} $$

Here, $\hat{r}$ is the radial unit vector and $\hat{z}$ is the unit vector along the axis of the rod.
