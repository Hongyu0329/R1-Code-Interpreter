The Poynting vector $\vec{S}$ is different inside and outside the rod. Let $r$ be the radial distance from the rod's axis, $\hat{r}$ be the radial unit vector, and $\hat{z}$ be the unit vector along the rod's axis.

**Inside the rod ($r \le R$):**
$$ \vec{S}_{in} = \frac{\rho^2 v r^2}{4\epsilon_0} \hat{z} - \frac{E \rho v r}{2} \hat{r} $$

**Outside the rod ($r > R$):**
$$ \vec{S}_{out} = \frac{\rho^2 v R^4}{4\epsilon_0 r^2} \hat{z} - \frac{E \rho v R^2}{2r} \hat{r} $$
