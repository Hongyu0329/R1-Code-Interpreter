The minimum of the asymmetric Brockett cost function $f: SO(n) \times SO(n) \to \mathbb{R}$, defined as $f(X, Y) = \langle A, X^\top B Y \rangle$, depends on the dimension $n$ of the matrices and the sign of the product of their determinants. Let $a_1 \geq a_2 \geq \dots \geq a_n \geq 0$ be the singular values of the $n \times n$ matrix $A$, and $b_1 \geq b_2 \geq \dots \geq b_n \geq 0$ be the singular values of the $n \times n$ matrix $B$. Furthermore, let $|A|$ and $|B|$ denote the determinants of $A$ and $B$, respectively.

The minimum value of the cost function is given by the following cases:

**Case 1: $n$ is even**

If the dimension $n$ is an even number, the minimum value of the function is:
$$-\sum_{i=1}^n a_i b_i$$

**Case 2: $n$ is odd**

If the dimension $n$ is an odd number, the minimum value depends on the sign of the product of the determinants of $A$ and $B$. Let $s = \text{sign}(|A||B|)$.

*   If $s \geq 0$ (i.e., the product of the determinants is non-negative), the minimum value is:
    $$-\sum_{i=1}^{n-1} a_i b_i + a_n b_n$$
*   If $s < 0$ (i.e., the product of the determinants is negative), the minimum value is:
    $$-\sum_{i=1}^n a_i b_i$$

This result can be summarized in a single formula using the Kronecker delta. Let $\delta_{n, \text{odd}}$ be 1 if $n$ is odd and 0 otherwise, and let $\delta_{s \geq 0}$ be 1 if $\text{sign}(|A||B|) \geq 0$ and 0 otherwise. The minimum is then:
$$-\sum_{i=1}^n a_i b_i + 2 \cdot \delta_{n, \text{odd}} \cdot \delta_{s \geq 0} \cdot a_n b_n$$
