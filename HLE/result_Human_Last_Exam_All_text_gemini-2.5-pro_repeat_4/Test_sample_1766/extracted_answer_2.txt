The common eigenvalues are found by considering the possible total angular momentum states. For a p-electron ($l=1, s=1/2$), the total angular momentum quantum number $j$ can be $j=3/2$ or $j=1/2$.

**Case 1: j = 3/2**
*   The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\hbar^2 j(j+1) = \frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$.
*   For this state, the possible values for the magnetic quantum number are $m_j = -3/2, -1/2, 1/2, 3/2$.
*   The corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\hbar m_j$, which are $-\frac{3}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar, \frac{1}{2}\hbar, \frac{3}{2}\hbar$.

**Case 2: j = 1/2**
*   The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\hbar^2 j(j+1) = \frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$.
*   For this state, the possible values for the magnetic quantum number are $m_j = -1/2, 1/2$.
*   The corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\hbar m_j$, which are $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar, \frac{1}{2}\hbar$.
