Sufficient conditions for the existence and uniqueness of an FGH-tripled fixed point `(x,y,z)` satisfying `F(x,y,z)=x`, `G(y,x,y)=y`, and `H(z,y,x)=z` are:

**1. Space Requirement:**
*   `(X, d_X)`, `(Y, d_Y)`, and `(Z, d_Z)` are non-empty **complete metric spaces**.

**2. Function (Lipschitz) Requirement:**
*   There exist non-negative constants `α1, α2, α3`, `β1, β2`, and `γ1, γ2, γ3` such that for all relevant points in the domains:
    *   `d_X(F(x1, y1, z1), F(x2, y2, z2)) ≤ α1*d_X(x1, x2) + α2*d_Y(y1, y2) + α3*d_Z(z1, z2)`
    *   `d_Y(G(y1, x1, y1), G(y2, x2, y2)) ≤ β1*d_Y(y1, y2) + β2*d_X(x1, x2)`
    *   `d_Z(H(z1, y1, x1), H(z2, y2, x2)) ≤ γ1*d_Z(z1, z2) + γ2*d_Y(y1, y2) + γ3*d_X(x1, x2)`

**3. Contraction Requirement:**
*   The sum of the Lipschitz constants must satisfy the inequality:
    `max( (α1 + β2 + γ3), (α2 + β1 + γ2), (α3 + γ1) ) < 1`
