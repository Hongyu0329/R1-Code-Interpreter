The common eigenvalues are found by calculating the allowed values of $\hbar^2 j(j+1)$ for $J^2$ and $\hbar m_j$ for $J_z$. For a p-electron, $l=1$ and $s=1/2$, which gives total angular momentum quantum numbers $j=3/2$ and $j=1/2$.

The pairs of (Eigenvalue of $J^2$, Eigenvalue of $J_z$) are:

For the $j=3/2$ multiplet:
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{3}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar)$

For the $j=1/2$ multiplet:
*   $(\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
