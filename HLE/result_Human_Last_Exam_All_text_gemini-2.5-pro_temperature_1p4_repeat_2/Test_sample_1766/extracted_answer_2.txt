For a p-electron ($l=1, s=1/2$), the possible total angular momentum quantum numbers are $j=3/2$ and $j=1/2$. The common eigenvalues of $(J^2, J_z)$ are:
For the $j=3/2$ multiplet, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$ and the eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar, \frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar$.
For the $j=1/2$ multiplet, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$ and the eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar$.
