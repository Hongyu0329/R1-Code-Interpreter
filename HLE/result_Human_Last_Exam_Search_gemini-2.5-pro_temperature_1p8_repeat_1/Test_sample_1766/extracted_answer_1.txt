For a p-electron, the common eigenvalues of $J^2$ and $J_z$ are:
1.  For j = 3/2, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, and the corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\pm\frac{1}{2}\hbar$ and $\pm\frac{3}{2}\hbar$.
2.  For j = 1/2, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$, and the corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\pm\frac{1}{2}\hbar$.
