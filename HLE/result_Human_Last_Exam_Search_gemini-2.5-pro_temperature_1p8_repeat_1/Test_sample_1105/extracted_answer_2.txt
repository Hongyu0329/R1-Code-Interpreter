The maximum distance \( l_{\text{max}} \) is the solution to the quadratic equation \( F l_{\text{max}}^2 + \left( \frac{1}{2}Mv_0^2 - F l_0 - \frac{GmM}{l_0} \right) l_{\text{max}} + GmM = 0 \). The physical solution can be found using the quadratic formula: \( l_{\text{max}} = \frac{-(\frac{1}{2}Mv_0^2 - F l_0 - \frac{GmM}{l_0}) + \sqrt{(\frac{1}{2}Mv_0^2 - F l_0 - \frac{GmM}{l_0})^2 - 4(F)(GmM)}}{2F} \), where the positive root is chosen to ensure \( l_{\text{max}} > l_0 \).
