Two languages from different Asian cultures with no direct contact that have highly similar words for "mom," "dad," and "broom" are Korean and Persian (Farsi).
- Mom: The Korean 'eomma' (엄마) is similar to the Persian 'mâmân' (مامان).
- Dad: The Korean 'appa' (아빠) is nearly identical to the Persian 'bâbâ' (بابا).
- Broom: The Korean word 'bitjaru' (빗자루) contains a morpheme, 'jaru', that is phonetically almost identical to the entire Persian word for broom, 'jâru' (جارو).
