The Lie group is defined by the four-dimensional Lie algebra spanned by the generators:
$X_1 = \partial_t$
$X_2 = \partial_x$
$X_3 = e^{k_1 t} \partial_x - \frac{k_1}{2} x u e^{k_1 t} \partial_u$
$X_4 = e^{k_1 t} u \partial_u$

The general representation of the transformation group on the coordinate $x$, denoted $\chi(\epsilon)$, is generated by an arbitrary element $a_1 X_1 + a_2 X_2 + a_3 X_3 + a_4 X_4$ of the Lie algebra. The transformation has two forms, depending on the parameter $a_1$:

**Case 1: For transformations involving time translation ($a_1 \neq 0$):**
$\chi = x + a_2 \epsilon + \frac{a_3 e^{k_1 t}}{k_1 a_1} (e^{k_1 a_1 \epsilon} - 1)$

**Case 2: For transformations not involving time translation ($a_1 = 0$):**
$\chi = x + (a_2 + a_3 e^{k_1 t}) \epsilon$

Here, $a_1, a_2, a_3$ are arbitrary real constants specifying the generator of the one-parameter subgroup, and $\epsilon$ is the group parameter.
