For case (a), the absorption cross-section equation is:
$$ \sigma_a(\omega_L) = N \frac{2 \pi^{1/2} \tau^{3/2} \omega_0 |\vec{\mu}_{ge} \cdot \vec{e}_p|^2}{\hbar c \epsilon_0} e^{-(\omega_0 - \omega_L)^2 \tau^2} $$
For case (b), the absorption cross-section equation is:
$$ \sigma_b(\omega_L) = N \frac{2 \pi^{1/2} \tau^{3/2} (\omega_0 + 2J/\hbar) |\vec{\mu}_{ge} \cdot \vec{e}_p|^2}{\hbar c \epsilon_0} e^{-\left(\omega_0 + \frac{2J}{\hbar} - \omega_L\right)^2 \tau^2} $$
where $N$ is the number of molecules, $\tau$ is the pulse duration parameter, $\omega_L$ is the laser frequency, $\omega_0$ is the single-molecule transition frequency, $\vec{\mu}_{ge}$ is the single-molecule transition dipole moment, $\vec{e}_p$ is the laser polarization vector, and $J$ is the near-neighbor coupling energy.
