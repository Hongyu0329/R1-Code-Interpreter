The function `f` and formula `C` are defined as follows.

Let the basic unit formula be `U = \bot \multimap \bot`.

The function `f` maps a natural number `w` to the formula:
$$
f(w) = \underbrace{U \otimes U \otimes \dots \otimes U}_{w \text{ times}} = \underbrace{(\bot \multimap \bot) \otimes \dots \otimes (\bot \multimap \bot)}_{w \text{ times}}
$$

The formula `C = C(W, m, b)` is constructed from a formula `B` that represents a single bin of capacity `b`. The formula `B` is defined as:
$$
B = (U^⊗b \multimap \bot) \multimap \bot = \left(\left(\underbrace{(\bot \multimap \bot) \otimes \dots \otimes (\bot \multimap \bot)}_{b \text{ times}}\right) \multimap \bot\right) \multimap \bot
$$
Then, the final formula `C` is the tensor product of `m` copies of `B`:
$$
C = \underbrace{B \otimes B \otimes \dots \otimes B}_{m \text{ times}} = \left( \left(\left(\underbrace{(\bot \multimap \bot) \otimes \dots \otimes (\bot \multimap \bot)}_{b \text{ times}}\right) \multimap \bot\right) \multimap \bot \right)^⊗m
$$
For `w=0`, `f(0)` is the empty tensor product, which is the constant `1`.
