The primary conditions for the existence and uniqueness of an FGH-tripled fixed point are:
1.  `(X, d_X)`, `(Y, d_Y)`, and `(Z, d_Z)` are non-empty complete metric spaces.
2.  The functions F, G, and H satisfy a joint contraction condition. For all `x1, x2 ∈ X`, `y1, y2 ∈ Y`, `z1, z2 ∈ Z`, there exist non-negative constants `α_i, β_i, γ_i` (i=1,2,3) such that:
    `d_X(F(x1,y1,z1), F(x2,y2,z2)) ≤ α_1d_X(x1,x2) + α_2d_Y(y1,y2) + α_3d_Z(z1,z2)`
    `d_Y(G(y1,z1,x1), G(y2,z2,x2)) ≤ β_1d_Y(y1,y2) + β_2d_Z(z1,z2) + β_3d_X(x1,x2)`
    `d_Z(H(z1,x1,y1), H(z2,x2,y2)) ≤ γ_1d_Z(z1,z2) + γ_2d_X(x1,x2) + γ_3d_Y(y1,y2)`
    with the sums of the constants being less than 1:
    `α_1+β_3+γ_2 < 1`
    `α_2+β_1+γ_3 < 1`
    `α_3+β_2+γ_1 < 1`
