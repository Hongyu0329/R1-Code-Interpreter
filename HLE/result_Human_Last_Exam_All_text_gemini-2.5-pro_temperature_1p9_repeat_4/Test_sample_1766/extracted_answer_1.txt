The problem has two sets of common eigenvalues corresponding to the two possible values of total angular momentum $j$:
1.  For the $j=3/2$ states: The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$. The corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$, and $-\frac{3}{2}\hbar$.
2.  For the $j=1/2$ states: The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$. The corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$ and $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$.
