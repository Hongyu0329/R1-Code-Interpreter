The problem has two sets of common eigenvalues, corresponding to the total angular momentum quantum numbers j=3/2 and j=1/2.

**1. For the j = 3/2 states:**
The common eigenvalues (Eigenvalue of $J^2$, Eigenvalue of $J_z$) are:
*   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $\frac{3}{2}\hbar$)
*   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
*   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
*   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $-\frac{3}{2}\hbar$)

**2. For the j = 1/2 states:**
The common eigenvalues (Eigenvalue of $J^2$, Eigenvalue of $J_z$) are:
*   ($\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
*   ($\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
