The system is defined by orbital quantum number $l=1$ and spin quantum number $s=1/2$. The total angular momentum quantum number $j$ can be $j=l+s=3/2$ or $j=|l-s|=1/2$.

The common eigenstates are denoted by $|j, m_j\rangle$, where $m_j$ is the total magnetic quantum number, with possible values from $-j$ to $j$. The eigenvalues of $J^2$ are $\hbar^2 j(j+1)$ and the eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\hbar m_j$.

The common eigenvalues are found by considering each possible value of $j$:

Case 1:  **j = 3/2**
The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\hbar^2 \frac{3}{2}(\frac{3}{2}+1) = \frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$.
For this $j$, the possible values of $m_j$ are $-3/2, -1/2, 1/2, 3/2$.
The pairs of (Eigenvalue of $J^2$, Eigenvalue of $J_z$) are:
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{3}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar)$

Case 2: **j = 1/2**
The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\hbar^2 \frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{2}+1) = \frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$.
For this $j$, the possible values of $m_j$ are $-1/2, 1/2$.
The pairs of (Eigenvalue of $J^2$, Eigenvalue of $J_z$) are:
*   $(\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
