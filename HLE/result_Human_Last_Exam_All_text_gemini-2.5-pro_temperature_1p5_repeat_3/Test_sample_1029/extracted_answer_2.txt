The Poynting vector $\vec{S}$ is given in cylindrical coordinates $(r, \phi, z)$ with unit vectors $(\hat{r}, \hat{\phi}, \hat{k})$ where the z-axis is the axis of the rod.

Inside the rod ($r < R$):
$$ \vec{S} = -\frac{E \rho v r}{2} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v r^2}{4\epsilon_0} \hat{k} $$

Outside the rod ($r > R$):
$$ \vec{S} = -\frac{E \rho v R^2}{2r} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v R^4}{4\epsilon_0 r^2} \hat{k} $$
