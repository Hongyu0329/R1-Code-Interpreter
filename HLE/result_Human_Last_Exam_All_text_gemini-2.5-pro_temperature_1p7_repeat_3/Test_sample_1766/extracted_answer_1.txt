For a p-electron ($l=1, s=1/2$), the total angular momentum quantum number $j$ can be $1/2$ or $3/2$. The eigenvalues of $J^2$ are $\hbar^2 j(j+1)$ and the eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\hbar m_j$.

The common eigenvalue pairs (Eigenvalue of $J^2$, Eigenvalue of $J_z$) are:

1.  For the $j=3/2$ multiplet:
    *   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $\frac{3}{2}\hbar$)
    *   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
    *   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
    *   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $-\frac{3}{2}\hbar$)

2.  For the $j=1/2$ multiplet:
    *   ($\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
    *   ($\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
