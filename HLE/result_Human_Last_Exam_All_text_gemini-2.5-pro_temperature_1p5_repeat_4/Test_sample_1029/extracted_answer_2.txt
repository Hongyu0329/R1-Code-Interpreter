The Poynting vector $\vec{S}$, in cylindrical coordinates $(r, \phi, z)$ with unit vectors $(\hat{r}, \hat{\phi}, \hat{k})$, is given by:
$\vec{S}(r) = \begin{cases} - \frac{E \rho v r}{2} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v r^2}{4\epsilon_0} \hat{k} & \text{for } r < R \\ - \frac{E \rho v R^2}{2r} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v R^4}{4\epsilon_0 r^2} \hat{k} & \text{for } r > R \end{cases}$
