The Poynting vector $\vec{S}$ is computed separately for the regions inside and outside the cylindrical rod.

**Inside the rod (for a radial distance $r < R$):**
$$ \vec{S} = -\frac{E \rho v r}{2} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v r^2}{4\epsilon_0} \hat{z} $$

**Outside the rod (for a radial distance $r > R$):**
$$ \vec{S} = -\frac{E \rho v R^2}{2r} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v R^4}{4\epsilon_0 r^2} \hat{z} $$

Here, $\hat{r}$ is the unit vector in the radial direction and $\hat{z}$ is the unit vector along the axis of the rod.
