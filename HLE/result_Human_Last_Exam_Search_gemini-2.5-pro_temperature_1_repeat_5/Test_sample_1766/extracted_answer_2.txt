For a p-electron, the orbital angular momentum quantum number is *l*=1 and the spin quantum number is *s*=1/2. The total angular momentum quantum number, *j*, can be *j* = *l* + *s* = 3/2 or *j* = |*l* - *s*| = 1/2.

The eigenvalues of $J^2$ are given by $j(j+1)\hbar^2$ and the eigenvalues of $J_z$ are given by $m_j\hbar$, where $m_j$ ranges from -*j* to *j*.

The common eigenvalues are:
For *j* = 3/2:
*   Eigenvalue of $J^2$: $(3/2)(3/2+1)\hbar^2 = (15/4)\hbar^2$
*   Eigenvalues of $J_z$: $m_j\hbar$ where $m_j \in \{-3/2, -1/2, 1/2, 3/2\}$.
    *   This gives the eigenvalue pairs: $((15/4)\hbar^2, -3/2\hbar)$, $((15/4)\hbar^2, -1/2\hbar)$, $((15/4)\hbar^2, 1/2\hbar)$, $((15/4)\hbar^2, 3/2\hbar)$.

For *j* = 1/2:
*   Eigenvalue of $J^2$: $(1/2)(1/2+1)\hbar^2 = (3/4)\hbar^2$
*   Eigenvalues of $J_z$: $m_j\hbar$ where $m_j \in \{-1/2, 1/2\}$.
    *   This gives the eigenvalue pairs: $((3/4)\hbar^2, -1/2\hbar)$, $((3/4)\hbar^2, 1/2\hbar)$.
