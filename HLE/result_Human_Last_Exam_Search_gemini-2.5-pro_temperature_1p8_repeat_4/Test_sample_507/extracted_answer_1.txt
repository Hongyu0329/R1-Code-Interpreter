Persian and Telugu are two languages from two different cultures in Asia where the words for "broom", "mom", and "dad" show notable similarities.

*   For "mom", the Persian word is **مامان (māmān)** and the Telugu word is **అమ్మ (amma)**.
*   For "dad", the Persian word is **بابا (bābā)**, which is similar to **అప్ప (appa)**, a term for father in Telugu.
*   For "broom", the Persian word is **جارو (jāru)** and the Telugu word is **చీపురు (chīpuru)**.
