A pair of Asian languages from different cultures with no direct contact that have nearly identical or highly similar words for "broom", "mom", and "dad" is Korean and Hindi/Urdu. In Korean, "mom" is "eomma" and "dad" is "appa", while in Hindi/Urdu, the informal terms are "ammā" and "abbā". The Korean word for "broom" is "bitjaru", which shares a phonetic resemblance with the Hindi/Urdu word "jhāṛū".
