The maximum distance \( l_{\text{max}} \) is given by the solution to the quadratic equation derived from the work-energy theorem. Let \( C = F l_0 + \frac{GmM}{l_0} - \frac{1}{2} M v_0^2 \). The maximum distance is the larger root of the quadratic equation \( F l_{\text{max}}^2 - C l_{\text{max}} + GmM = 0 \).

The final expression for the maximum distance is:
\( l_{\text{max}} = \frac{(F l_0 + \frac{GmM}{l_0} - \frac{1}{2} M v_0^2) + \sqrt{(F l_0 + \frac{GmM}{l_0} - \frac{1}{2} M v_0^2)^2 - 4FGmM}}{2F} \)
