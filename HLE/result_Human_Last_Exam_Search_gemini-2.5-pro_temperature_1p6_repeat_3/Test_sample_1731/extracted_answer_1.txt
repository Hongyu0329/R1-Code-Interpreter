**Mean Energy (U):** The equilibrium mean energy is given by the Stefan-Boltzmann law, U ∝ V T<sup>4</sup>, where V is the volume and T is the temperature. Specifically, U = (8π<sup>5</sup>k<sub>B</sub><sup>4</sup> / 15(hc)<sup>3</sup>) V T<sup>4</sup>.

**Entropy (S):** The equilibrium entropy is S ∝ V T<sup>3</sup>. Specifically, S = (32π<sup>5</sup>k<sub>B</sub><sup>4</sup> / 45(hc)<sup>3</sup>) V T<sup>3</sup>.
