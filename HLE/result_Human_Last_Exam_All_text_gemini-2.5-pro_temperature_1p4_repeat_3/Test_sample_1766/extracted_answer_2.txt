The common eigenvalues are presented as pairs (eigenvalue of $J^2$, eigenvalue of $J_z$).
There are two possible values for the total angular momentum quantum number, $j$: $3/2$ and $1/2$.

1.  For the states where $j = 3/2$, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$. The possible eigenvalues for $J_z$ are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar, \frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar$. The common eigenvalues are:
    *   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $\frac{3}{2}\hbar$)
    *   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
    *   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
    *   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $-\frac{3}{2}\hbar$)

2.  For the states where $j = 1/2$, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$. The possible eigenvalues for $J_z$ are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar$. The common eigenvalues are:
    *   ($\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
    *   ($\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
