The expectation of $\tau$ is $\frac{3}{50} \sum_{1 \le i < j \le 5} (x_j(0) - x_i(0))^2$, where $x_i(0)$ are the initial positions of the five particles. Let the initial separations be $d_1=N_1, d_2=M_1, d_3=N_2, d_4=M_2$. Then the initial sum of squared distances is $S_2(0) = d_1^2 + (d_1+d_2)^2 + (d_1+d_2+d_3)^2 + (d_1+d_2+d_3+d_4)^2 + d_2^2 + (d_2+d_3)^2 + (d_2+d_3+d_4)^2 + d_3^2 + (d_3+d_4)^2 + d_4^2$. Expanding this gives: $S_2(0) = 4N_1^2 + 6M_1^2 + 6N_2^2 + 4M_2^2 + 6N_1M_1 + 4N_1N_2 + 2N_1M_2 + 6M_1N_2 + 4M_1M_2 + 6N_2M_2$. The expectation is $E[\tau] = \frac{3}{50}S_2(0)$.
