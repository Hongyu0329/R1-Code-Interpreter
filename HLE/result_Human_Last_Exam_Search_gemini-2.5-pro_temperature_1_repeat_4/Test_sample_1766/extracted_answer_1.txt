The common eigenvalues of $J^2$ and $J_z$ are determined by the possible values of the total angular momentum quantum number, $j$, and the magnetic quantum number, $m_j$. For a p-electron, $l=1$ and $s=1/2$, leading to $j=3/2$ and $j=1/2$.
For $j=3/2$, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, and the eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar, \frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar$.
For $j=1/2$, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$, and the eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar$.
