The Lie group of infinitesimal transformations that leaves the equation $u_t = u_{xx} + (k_1\ln(u) +k_2)u$ invariant is generated by the following four-dimensional Lie algebra. The general infinitesimal generator, parameterized by arbitrary constants $c_1, c_2, c_3, c_4$, is:
$X = c_1 \frac{\partial}{\partial t} + \left(c_4 - \frac{2c_2}{k_1} e^{k_1 t}\right) \frac{\partial}{\partial x} + e^{k_1 t} (c_2 x + c_3) u \frac{\partial}{\partial u}$

The representation of the finite transformation on the coordinate $x$, which is obtained by integrating the flow $\frac{d\bar{x}}{d\epsilon} = \xi^2(\bar{t})$ with the group parameter $\epsilon$, is given in two distinct forms depending on the value of the constant $c_1$.

**Case 1: $c_1 \neq 0$**
The transformation on $x$ is:
$\bar{x} = x + c_4 \epsilon - \frac{2c_2}{k_1^2 c_1} e^{k_1 t} (e^{k_1 c_1 \epsilon} - 1)$
