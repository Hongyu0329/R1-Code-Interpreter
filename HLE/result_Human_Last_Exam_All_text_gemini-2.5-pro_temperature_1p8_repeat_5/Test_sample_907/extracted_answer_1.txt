**a) Interaction neglected:**
The absorption cross-section is the incoherent sum of the absorptions of N independent molecules. It exhibits a single absorption peak at the excitation frequency of an individual molecule, $\omega_{exc}$. The equation is:
$$ \sigma_a(\omega) = \frac{\pi\omega N |\vec{\mu} \cdot \hat{\epsilon}|^2}{\epsilon_0 n c \hbar} \delta\left(\omega - \frac{E_{exc}}{\hbar}\right) $$
Here, $N$ is the number of molecules, $\vec{\mu}$
