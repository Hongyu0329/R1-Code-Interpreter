The one-step transformation of the probability distribution is given by $P_{t+1}(i) = \frac{1}{2} P_t(i-1) + \frac{1}{2} P_t(i+1)$, where indices are modulo $N$. The transition probability matrix is $\hat{A}$ with elements $A_{ij} = \frac{1}{2}(\delta_{i, j+1 \pmod N} + \delta_{i, j-1 \pmod N})$. The eigenvectors are $v_n$ with components $[v_n]_j = e^{ij(2\pi n/N)}$ and the eigenvalues are $\lambda_n = \cos\left(\frac{2\pi n}{N}\right)$ for $n=0, 1, \dots, N-1$. The second-largest eigenvalue is $\lambda_1 = \cos\left(\frac{2\pi}{N}\right)$. The rate of relaxation determined by this eigenvalue is $\gamma = -\ln(\lambda_1) = -\ln\left(\cos\left(\frac{2\pi}{N}\right)\right)$.
