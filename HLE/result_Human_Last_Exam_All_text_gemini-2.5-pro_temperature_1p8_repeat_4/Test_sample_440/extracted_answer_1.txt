The density profile \(\rho(z)\) of the gas mixture as a function of height \(z\) is given by:
\[ \rho(z) = m_A n_A(z) + m_B n_B(z) \]
where the number density profiles for each gas, \(n_A(z)\) and \(n_B(z)\), are determined by the simultaneous solution of the following system of two coupled transcendental equations and two integral constraints.

**Governing Equations for \(n_A(z)\) and \(n_B(z)\):**
1.  \( k_B T \ln\left(\frac{n_A(z)}{1 - n_A(z) b'_{AA} - n_B(z) b'_{BB}}\right) + \frac{(n_A(z)+n_B(z)) k_B T b'_{AA}}{1 - (n_A(z) b'_{AA} + n_B(z) b'_{BB})} - 2(a'_{AA} n_A(z) + a'_{AB} n_B(z)) + m_A g z = C_A \)
2.  \( k_B T \ln\left(\frac{n_B(z)}{1 - n_A(z) b'_{AA} - n_B(z) b'_{BB}}\right) + \frac{(n_A(z)+n_B(z)) k_B T b'_{BB}}{1 - (n_A(z) b'_{AA} + n_B(z) b'_{BB})} - 2(a'_{BB} n_B(z) + a'_{AB} n_A(z)) + m_B g z = C_B \)

**Constraints to find constants \(C_A\) and \(C_B\):**
1.  \( A \int_0^H n_A(z) \, dz = N_A \)
2.  \( A \int_0^H n_B(z) \, dz = N_B \)

The per-particle parameters \(m_i\), \(a'_{ij}\), and \(b'_{ii}\) are obtained by converting the given molar parameters using Avogadro's number. This system of equations implicitly defines the density profile.
