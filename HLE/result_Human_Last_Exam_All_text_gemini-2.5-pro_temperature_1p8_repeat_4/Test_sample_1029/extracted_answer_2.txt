The Poynting vector $\vec{S}$ is given in cylindrical coordinates $(r, \phi, z)$ with the z-axis along the rod's axis as:
For inside the rod ($r \le R$):
$$ \vec{S}(r \le R) = -\frac{E\rho v r}{2} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v r^2}{4\epsilon_0} \hat{k} $$
For outside the rod ($r > R$):
$$ \vec{S}(r > R) = -\frac{E\rho v R^2}{2r} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v R^4}{4\epsilon_0 r^2} \hat{k} $$
where $\hat{r}$ and $\hat{k}$ are the unit vectors in the radial and axial directions, respectively.
