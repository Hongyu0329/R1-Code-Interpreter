The Poynting vector $\vec{S}$ depends on the radial distance $r$ from the axis of the rod. It has different forms inside and outside the rod. Using a cylindrical coordinate system $(r, \phi, z)$ with the z-axis along the rod's axis:

**Inside the rod ($r < R$):**
$$ \vec{S} = \frac{\rho^2 v r^2}{4\epsilon_0} \hat{k} - \frac{E \rho v r}{2} \hat{r} $$

**Outside the rod ($r > R$):**
$$ \vec{S} = \frac{\rho^2 v R^4}{4\epsilon_0 r^2} \hat{k} - \frac{E \rho v R^2}{2r} \hat{r} $$
