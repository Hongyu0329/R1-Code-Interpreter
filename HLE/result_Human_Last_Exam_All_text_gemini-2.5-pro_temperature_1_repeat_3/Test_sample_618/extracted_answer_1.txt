The general representation for the Lie group transformation on the spatial coordinate $x$, denoted by $x^*$, is given in terms of the initial coordinate $x$, time $t$, the group parameter $\epsilon$, and arbitrary constants $c_1, c_2, c_3$ (which select a specific transformation from the group). There are two forms depending on the constant $c_3$:

1.  If $c_3 \neq 0$:
    $x^* = x + \frac{c_1 e^{k_1 t}}{k_1 c_3}(e^{k_1 c_3 \epsilon} - 1) + c_2 \epsilon$

2.  If $c_3 = 0$:
    $x^* = x + (c_1 e^{k_1 t} + c_2)\epsilon$
