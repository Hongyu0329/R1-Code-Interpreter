
**Equilibrium Mean Energy ($E_{eq}$):**

The equilibrium mean energy is the sum of the average energy in each energy level $\epsilon_i$ (with degeneracy $g_i$), given by the Bose-Einstein distribution with chemical potential $\mu=0$:

$E_{eq} = \sum_i \frac{g_i \epsilon_i}{e^{\epsilon_i / k_B T} - 1}$

where:
*   $\epsilon_i$ is the energy of the i-th level.
*   $g_i$ is the degeneracy of the i-th level.
*   $k_B$ is the Boltzmann constant.
*   $T$ is the equilibrium temperature.

**Equilibrium Entropy ($S_{eq}$):**

The equilibrium entropy is the value of the statistical entropy for a Bose gas evaluated at the equilibrium occupation numbers. Let $\nu_{i,eq} = (e^{\epsilon_i / k_B T} - 1)^{-1}$ be the average occupation per state at energy level $\epsilon_i$. The entropy is given by:

$S_{eq} = k_B \sum_i g_i \left[ (1 + \nu_{i, eq}) \log(1 + \nu_{i, eq}) - \nu_{i, eq} \log \nu_{i, eq} \right]$

Alternatively, using thermodynamic relations, the entropy can be expressed as:

$S_{eq} = \frac{E_{eq}}{T} - k_B \sum_i g_i \log\left(1 - e^{-\epsilon_i / k_B T}\right)$
