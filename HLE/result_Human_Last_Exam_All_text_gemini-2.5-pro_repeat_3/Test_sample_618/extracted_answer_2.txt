The general representation for the finite transformation on x is given by two forms, depending on the constant $c_4$ from the time-translation infinitesimal $\tau=c_4$. Let $\epsilon$ be the group parameter.
Case 1: For transformations involving time shifts ($c_4 \neq 0$):
$x^* = x + c_2 \epsilon + \frac{c_1}{k_1^2 c_4} e^{k_1 t} (e^{k_1 c_4 \epsilon} - 1)$
Case 2: For transformations without time shifts ($c_4 = 0$):
$x^* = x + \left(c_2 + \frac{c_1}{k_1} e^{k_1 t}\right)\epsilon$
Here, $c_1$ and $c_2$ are arbitrary constants from the spatial transformation infinitesimal $\xi = c_2 + \frac{c_1}{k_1} e^{k_1 t}$.
