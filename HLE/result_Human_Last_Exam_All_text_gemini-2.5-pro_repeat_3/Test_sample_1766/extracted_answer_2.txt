The common eigenvalues are pairs of (Eigenvalue of $J^2$, Eigenvalue of $J_z$).
For j=3/2, the J^2 eigenvalue is 15/4 * h_bar^2, and the J_z eigenvalues are 3/2 * h_bar, 1/2 * h_bar, -1/2 * h_bar, -3/2 * h_bar. This gives four pairs of common eigenvalues: (15/4 * h_bar^2, 3/2 * h_bar), (15/4 * h_bar^2, 1/2 * h_bar), (15/4 * h_bar^2, -1/2 * h_bar), (15/4 * h_bar^2, -3/2 * h_bar).
For j=1/2, the J^2 eigenvalue is 3/4 * h_bar^2, and the J_z eigenvalues are 1/2 * h_bar, -1/2 * h_bar. This gives two pairs of common eigenvalues: (3/4 * h_bar^2, 1/2 * h_bar), (3/4 * h_bar^2, -1/2 * h_bar).
