The Poynting vector $\vec{S}$ is given in cylindrical coordinates $(r, \phi, z)$, where the z-axis is the axis of the rod, $\hat{r}$ is the radial unit vector and $\hat{k}$ is the axial unit vector.
For $r < R$ (inside the rod):
$$ \vec{S}(r) = - \frac{E \rho v r}{2} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v r^2}{4 \epsilon_0} \hat{k} $$
For $r > R$ (outside the rod):
$$ \vec{S}(r) = - \frac{E \rho v R^2}{2r} \hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v R^4}{4 \epsilon_0 r^2} \hat{k} $$
