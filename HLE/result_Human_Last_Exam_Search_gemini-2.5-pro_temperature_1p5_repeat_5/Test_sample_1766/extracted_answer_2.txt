For a p-electron, the common eigenvalues of $J^2$ and $J_z$ are given by the pairs $(j(j+1)\hbar^2, m_j\hbar)$.
For $j=1/2$, the eigenvalues are $((3/4)\hbar^2, 1/2\hbar)$ and $((3/4)\hbar^2, -1/2\hbar)$.
For $j=3/2$, the eigenvalues are $((15/4)\hbar^2, 3/2\hbar)$, $((15/4)\hbar^2, 1/2\hbar)$, $((15/4)\hbar^2, -1/2\hbar)$, and $((15/4)\hbar^2, -3/2\hbar)$.
