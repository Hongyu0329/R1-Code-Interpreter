**Function f:**
Let G be the formula `⊥ ⊸ 1`. The function `f` maps a natural number `w` to the formula `f(w) = G^⊗w`, where `G^⊗w` denotes the formula `G ⊗ G ⊗ ... ⊗ G` (`w` times). For `w=0`, `f(0) = 1`.

**Formula C:**
Using the same formula `G = ⊥ ⊸ 1`, the formula `C(W, m, b)` is `(G^⊗b)^⊗m`, which denotes the formula `(G^⊗b) ⊗ (G^⊗b) ⊗ ... ⊗ (G^⊗b)` (`m` times). This is equivalent to `G^⊗(mb)`.
