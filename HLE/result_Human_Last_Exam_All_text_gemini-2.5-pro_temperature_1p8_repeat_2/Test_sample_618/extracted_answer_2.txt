The general finite transformation on $x$, denoted by $x^*$, is obtained by integrating the flow generated by the infinitesimal vector field. Let the group parameter be $\epsilon$, and let $C_1, C_2, C_4$ be arbitrary constants corresponding to the generators that affect the coordinates $t$ and $x$. The transformation has two possible forms, depending on whether the constant $C_1$ (related to time translation) is zero.

Case 1: If $C_1 \neq 0$:
$x^* = x + C_2\epsilon - \frac{2C_4}{k_1^2 C_1} e^{k_1 t} (e^{k_1 C_1 \epsilon} - 1)$

Case 2: If $C_1 = 0$:
$x^* = x + \left( C_2 - \frac{2C_4}{k_1} e^{k_1 t} \right) \epsilon$
