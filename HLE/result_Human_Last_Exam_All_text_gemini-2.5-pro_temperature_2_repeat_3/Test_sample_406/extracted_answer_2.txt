An FGH-tripled fixed point exists and is unique if (1) The spaces (X, d_X), (Y, d_Y), and (Z, d_Z) are complete metric spaces. (2) There exist non-negative constants α_i, β_i, γ_i for i=1,2,3 such that for all relevant points, the functions satisfy the joint Lipschitz-like conditions: d_X(F(x,y,z), F(u,v,w)) ≤ α₁d_X(x,u)+β₁d_Y(y,v)+γ₁d_Z(z,w), d_Y(G(y,x,p), G(v,u,q)) ≤ α₂d_X(x,u)+β₂d_Y(y,v)+γ₂d_Y(p,q), and d_Z(H(z,y,x), H(w,v,u)) ≤ α₃d_X(x,u)+β₃d_Y(y,v)+γ₃d_Z(z,w). (3) The combined contraction factor K = max{(α₁+α₂+α₃), (β₁+β₂+γ₂+β₃), (γ₁+γ₃)} is strictly less than 1.
