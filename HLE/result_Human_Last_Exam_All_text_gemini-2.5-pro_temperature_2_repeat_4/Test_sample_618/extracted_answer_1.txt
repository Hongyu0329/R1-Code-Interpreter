The general representation for the transformations on x is given by two forms, depending on the constant $c_1$ from the infinitesimal generator $\tau = c_1$:

1. If $c_1 \neq 0$:
$\bar{x} = x + c_3 \epsilon + \frac{c_2}{k_1^2 c_1} e^{k_1 t} (e^{k_1 c_1 \epsilon} - 1)$

2. If $c_1 = 0$:
$\bar{x} = x + \left(c_3 + \frac{c_2}{k_1} e^{k_1 t}\right) \epsilon$

Here, $\epsilon$ is the group parameter and $c_2, c_3$ are arbitrary constants from the infinitesimal generator for $x$, $\xi(t) = c_3 + \frac{c_2}{k_1} e^{k_1 t}$.
