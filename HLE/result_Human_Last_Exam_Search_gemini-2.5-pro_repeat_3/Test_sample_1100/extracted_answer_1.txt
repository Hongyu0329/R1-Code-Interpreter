The new food is important because it is likely rich in fava beans, which contain high levels of L-DOPA, the precursor to dopamine. This dietary change is likely an attempt to naturally increase her dopamine levels to combat the negative symptoms of her schizophrenia (lack of interest, poverty of speech), which are associated with low dopamine and may have been worsened by her antipsychotic medication and the withdrawal of a second, likely dopamine-promoting, drug.
