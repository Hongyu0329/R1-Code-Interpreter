The common eigenvalues of $J^2$ and $J_z$ are the pairs $(\hbar^2 j(j+1), \hbar m_j)$. For a p-electron ($l=1, s=1/2$), the possible values for the total angular momentum quantum number are $j=3/2$ and $j=1/2$.

For $j=3/2$, the eigenvalues are:
-   $J^2$: $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$
-   $J_z$: $\frac{3}{2}\hbar, \frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar$

For $j=1/2$, the eigenvalues are:
-   $J^2$: $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$
-   $J_z$: $\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar$
