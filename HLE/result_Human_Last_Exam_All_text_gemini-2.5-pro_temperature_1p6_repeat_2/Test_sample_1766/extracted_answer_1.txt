The common eigenvalues are pairs of the form (Eigenvalue of $J^2$, Eigenvalue of $J_z$). There are two groups of solutions corresponding to the two possible total angular momentum quantum numbers, $j=3/2$ and $j=1/2$.

For the j=3/2 states:
The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$. The eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar, \frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar$.
This gives four common eigenvalue pairs:
$(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{3}{2}\hbar)$, $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar)$, $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar)$, $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar)$

For the j=1/2 states:
The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$. The eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar$.
This gives two common eigenvalue pairs:
$(\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar)$, $(\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
