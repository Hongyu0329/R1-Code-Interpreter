The Poynting vector $\vec{S}$ depends on the radial distance $r$ from the axis of the rod. Let $\hat{r}$ be the unit vector pointing radially outward from the axis and $\hat{k}$ be the unit vector along the axis in the direction of motion.

Inside the rod ($r < R$):
$$ \vec{S}(r) = -\frac{E\rho v r}{2}\hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v r^2}{4\epsilon_0}\hat{k} $$

Outside the rod ($r > R$):
$$ \vec{S}(r) = -\frac{E\rho v R^2}{2r}\hat{r} + \frac{\rho^2 v R^4}{4\epsilon_0 r^2}\hat{k} $$
