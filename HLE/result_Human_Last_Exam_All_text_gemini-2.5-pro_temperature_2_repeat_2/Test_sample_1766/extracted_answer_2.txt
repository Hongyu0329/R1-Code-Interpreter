The problem involves states described by quantum numbers $|j, m_j\rangle$. There are two possible values for $j$: $3/2$ and $1/2$.
1.  For the $j = 3/2$ manifold:
    The common eigenstates are $|3/2, 3/2\rangle$, $|3/2, 1/2\rangle$, $|3/2, -1/2\rangle$, and $|3/2, -3/2\rangle$.
    For all these states, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\hbar^2 \frac{3}{2}(\frac{3}{2}+1) = \frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$.
    The corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$, and $-\frac{3}{2}\hbar$, respectively.
2.  For the $j = 1/2$ manifold:
    The common eigenstates are $|1/2, 1/2\rangle$ and $|1/2, -1/2\rangle$.
    For these two states, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\hbar^2 \frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{2}+1) = \frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$.
    The corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$ and $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$, respectively.
