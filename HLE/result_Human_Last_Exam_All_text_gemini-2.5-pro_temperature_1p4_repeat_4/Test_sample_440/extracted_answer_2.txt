\(\rho(z) = m_A n_A(z) + m_B n_B(z)\)

where \(n_A(z)\) and \(n_B(z)\) are the solutions to the system of equations:
$$
\begin{cases}
n_A(z) = C_A \exp \left( \frac{-m_A g z + 2(a'_{AA} n_A(z) + a'_{AB} n_B(z))}{k_B T} \right) \\
n_B(z) = C_B \exp \left( \frac{-m_B g z + 2(a'_{AB} n_A(z) + a'_{BB} n_B(z))}{k_B T} \right)
\end{cases}
$$
The constants \(C_A\) and \(C_B\) are determined by the normalization conditions, which ensure the total number of particles of each species is correct:
$$
N_A = A \int_0^H n_A(z) \, dz \quad \text{and} \quad N_B = A \int_0^H n_B(z) \, dz
$$
The physical parameters in these equations are defined as:
*   \(m_i = M_i / N_{Av}\): mass of a single particle of species \(i\).
*   \(a'_{ij} = a_{ij} / N_{Av}^2\): per-particle van der Waals interaction coefficients.
*   \(g\), \(T\), \(A\), \(H\), \(N_A\), \(N_B\): given physical parameters.
*   \(k_B\): Boltzmann constant.
*   \(N_{Av}\): Avogadro's number.
