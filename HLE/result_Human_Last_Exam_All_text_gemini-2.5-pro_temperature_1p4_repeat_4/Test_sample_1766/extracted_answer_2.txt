The common eigenvalues are given by the pair (Eigenvalue of $J^2$, Eigenvalue of $J_z$).
There are two sets of solutions corresponding to the total angular momentum quantum numbers $j=3/2$ and $j=1/2$.

For $j=3/2$, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$. The possible eigenvalues for $J_z$ are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar, \frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar$. This gives the pairs:
$(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{3}{2}\hbar)$, $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar)$, $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar)$, $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar)$

For $j=1/2$, the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$. The possible eigenvalues for $J_z$ are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar$. This gives the pairs:
$(\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar)$, $(\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
