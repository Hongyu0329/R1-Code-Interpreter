The common eigenvalues are pairs of ($J^2$ eigenvalue, $J_z$ eigenvalue).
For $j=3/2$, the $J^2$ eigenvalue is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$. The corresponding $J_z$ eigenvalues are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar, \frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar$.
For $j=1/2$, the $J^2$ eigenvalue is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$. The corresponding $J_z$ eigenvalues are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar$.
This gives the following six pairs of common eigenvalues:
($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $\frac{3}{2}\hbar$), ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$), ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$), ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$, $-\frac{3}{2}\hbar$), ($\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$), ($\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$).
