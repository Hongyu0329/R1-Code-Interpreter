In the frequency-eigenstate representation $(k, \omega)$, the bare Green's function $G_0(k, \omega)$ is inversely proportional to the term $(\hbar\omega - \epsilon_k)$. The single-particle energy eigenvalue $\epsilon_k$ determines the position of a simple pole of the Green's function in the complex frequency plane. The functional form is $G_0(k, \omega) = \frac{1}{\hbar\omega - \epsilon_k + i\delta}$, where $i\delta$ is an infinitesimal imaginary part required for causality.
