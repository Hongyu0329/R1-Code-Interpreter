There are two possible eigenvalues for $J^2$: $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$ and $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$.
If the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$ (corresponding to j=3/2), the possible eigenvalues for $J_z$ are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar, \frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar$.
If the eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$ (corresponding to j=1/2), the possible eigenvalues for $J_z$ are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar$.
