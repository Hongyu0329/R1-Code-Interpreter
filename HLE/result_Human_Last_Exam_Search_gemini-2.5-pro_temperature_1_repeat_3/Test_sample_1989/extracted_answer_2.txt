The corrector is the factor $r^{A(1-\cos\theta)+B\sin\theta} e^{B\theta}$. This factor multiplies the asymptotic behavior for the $A=B=0$ case. Thus, the large-distance behavior of $\omega$ is given by:
$$
\omega(r,\theta) \sim C(\theta) \frac{1}{r^{1/2}}e^{-r(1-\cos\theta)} \cdot r^{A(1-\cos\theta)+B\sin\theta} e^{B\theta}
$$
where $C(\theta)$ is a function of the angle that depends on the source term $f$.
