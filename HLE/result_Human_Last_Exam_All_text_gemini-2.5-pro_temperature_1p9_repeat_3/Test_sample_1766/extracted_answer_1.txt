For a p-electron ($l=1, s=1/2$), the total angular momentum quantum number $j$ can be $j = 3/2$ or $j = 1/2$. The common eigenvalues of $J^2$ and $J_z$ are the pairs ($\hbar^2 j(j+1), \hbar m_j$) corresponding to the common eigenstates $|j, m_j\rangle$.

The sets of common eigenvalues are:
1.  **For j = 3/2:** The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$. The possible eigenvalues for $J_z$ are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar$, $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$, $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$, and $-\frac{3}{2}\hbar$.
    This gives the eigenvalue pairs:
    *   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{3}{2}\hbar$)
    *   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
    *   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
    *   ($\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar$)

2.  **For j = 1/2:** The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$. The possible eigenvalues for $J_z$ are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar$ and $-\frac{1}{2}\hbar$.
    This gives the eigenvalue pairs:
    *   ($\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
    *   ($\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar$)
