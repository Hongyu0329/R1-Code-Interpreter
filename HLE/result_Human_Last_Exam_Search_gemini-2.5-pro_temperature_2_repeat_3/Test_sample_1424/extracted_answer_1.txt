In the Feynman path integral formalism, the bare Green's function \(G_0\) depends on the single-particle energy eigenvalues \(\epsilon_k\) as an inverse function in frequency-momentum space. Specifically, for a given momentum \(k\) and frequency \(\omega\), the bare Green's function \(G_0(k, \omega)\) is proportional to \((\omega - \epsilon_k)^{-1}\), where the eigenvalues \(\epsilon_k\) determine the poles of the function.
