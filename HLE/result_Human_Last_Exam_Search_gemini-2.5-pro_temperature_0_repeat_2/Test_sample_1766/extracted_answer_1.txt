For a p-electron, the possible common eigenvalues for $J^2$ and $J_z$ are:
For the state with total angular momentum quantum number **j = 3/2**:
The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is **(15/4)ħ²**.
The corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are **-3/2 ħ, -1/2 ħ, 1/2 ħ, and 3/2 ħ**.

For the state with total angular momentum quantum number **j = 1/2**:
The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is **(3/4)ħ²**.
The corresponding eigenvalues of $J_z$ are **-1/2 ħ and 1/2 ħ**.
