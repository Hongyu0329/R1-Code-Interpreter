The solutions for $z \in \mathbb{C}$ are given by the set $z_{k,n} = i \frac{W_n(-(\frac{\pi}{2} + 2k\pi))}{\frac{\pi}{2} + 2k\pi}$ for all integers $k, n$. This set includes:
1. Two real solutions: $z=1$ and $z=-1$.
2. An infinite sequence of purely imaginary solutions $z_k = i \frac{W_0(-(\frac{\pi}{2} + 2k\pi))}{\frac{\pi}{2} + 2k\pi}$ for each integer $k \le -1$.
3. A doubly infinite set of other complex solutions for all other pairs of $(k,n)$.
