The common eigenvalues are found by considering the two possible total angular momentum states, $j=3/2$ and $j=1/2$.

For the j = 3/2 state (4 degenerate states):
The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2$. The eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{3}{2}\hbar, \frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar$.
The corresponding common eigenvalue pairs are:
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{3}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{15}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{3}{2}\hbar)$

For the j = 1/2 state (2 degenerate states):
The eigenvalue of $J^2$ is $\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2$. The eigenvalues of $J_z$ are $\frac{1}{2}\hbar, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar$.
The corresponding common eigenvalue pairs are:
*   $(\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, \frac{1}{2}\hbar)$
*   $(\frac{3}{4}\hbar^2, -\frac{1}{2}\hbar)
