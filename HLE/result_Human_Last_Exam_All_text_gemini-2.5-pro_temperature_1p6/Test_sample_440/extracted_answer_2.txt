The density profile \(\rho(z)\) is given by the sum of an ideal gas term and a correction term for non-ideal interactions:
\[ \rho(z) = \left[ m_A n_A^{(0)}(z) + m_B n_B^{(0)}(z) \right] + \frac{2}{kT}\left[ m_A a'_{AA} (n_A^{(0)}(z))^2 + m_B a'_{BB} (n_B^{(0)}(z))^2 + (m_A a'_{AB} + m_B a'_{AB}) n_A^{(0)}(z) n_B^{(0)}(z) \right] \]
where \(n_i^{(0)}(z)\) is the ideal gas number density profile for species \(i\):
\[ n_i^{(0)}(z) = C_i^{(0)} \exp\left(-\frac{m_i g z}{kT}\right) \]
The constants and parameters are defined as:
- \(m_i = M_i / N_{av}\) (particle mass)
- \(a'_{ij} = a_{ij} / N_{av}^2\) (per-particle-pair van der Waals attraction parameter)
- \(k\) is the Boltzmann constant, \(g\) is gravitational acceleration, \(T\) is temperature.
- \(C_i^{(0)}\) are the normalization constants determined by the total particle numbers \(N_i\), container area \(A\), and height \(H\):
\[ C_i^{(0)} = \frac{N_i}{A} \frac{m_i g / kT}{1 - \exp(-m_i g H / kT)} \]
