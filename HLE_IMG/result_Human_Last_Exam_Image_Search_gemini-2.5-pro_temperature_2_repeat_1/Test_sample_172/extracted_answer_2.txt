
**Formulation of Total Power Delivered:**
The total power delivered to the grid ($P_{\text{grid}}$) as a function of the given power sources and compensation is:
$P_{\text{grid}}(P_c) = (P^{0}_{\text{wind}} + P_{\text{wave}} + P_c) - 0.01 \times (P^{0}_{\text{wind}} + P_{\text{wave}} + P_c)^2$
Substituting the values $P^{0}_{\text{wind}} = 10$ MW and $P_{\text{wave}} = 5$ MW:
$P_{\text{grid}}(P_c) = (15 + P_c) - 0.01 \times (15 + P_c)^2$

**Optimal Compensation Power ($P{c}^{\text{opt}}$):**
To meet the grid demand of at least 12 MW while minimizing cable losses, the optimal compensation power required is the minimum feasible value that satisfies the constraints.
$P{c}^{\text{opt}} = 35 - 10\sqrt{13} \approx -1.056 \text{ MW}$

**Total Power Delivered to the Grid:**
With the optimal compensation power, the system delivers the minimum required power to the onshore grid:
$P_{\text{grid}} = 12 \text{ MW}$
